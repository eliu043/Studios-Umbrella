A.I. Toys

Ani Liu's algorithmically generated toy series turns gendered consumer data into evidence. The work asks what happens when a machine learns from toys already sorted into "girls" and "boys."

Artist page Digital series Unboxing Mania
central_argument.txt

AI does not invent gender stereotypes from nowhere. It learns them from the culture we feed it.

A.I. Toys uses machine learning to transform real toy-market data into absurd new toy concepts. The generated toys are funny because they are recognizable: their logic comes from actual product categories, marketing language, and social expectations.

Girls dataset

Jewelry / domestic chores / dolls / animals

Pink appears here as a quoted consumer signal: not an endorsement, but a marker of the narrow roles repeatedly attached to toys marketed toward girls.

Boys dataset

Weaponry / electronics / cars / construction

Blue appears as the parallel quoted signal, pointing to how power, machines, building, and conflict are packaged as masculine play.

artist_words.html

Gender is constructed through objects of play.

The toys described on Liu's screens were generated by a machine learning algorithm trained on real online consumer products labeled as "girls" and "boys" toys. Liu frames the resulting objects as reflections of the values adults place on children through design, marketing, and play.

Source: Liu, Ani. "A.I. Toys (MEGA HAUL RANDOM and MIXED SURPRISE ULTRA RARE UNBOXING COMPILATION!!!)." Ani Liu, 2022.

visual_analysis.gif

Consumer culture becomes a database interface.

The work borrows from product listings, toy packaging, unboxing videos, and screen-based browsing. A minimal Webcore structure lets the page echo online catalogs and early web indexes without burying the critique in visual noise.

research_questions.url

Questions for the visitor

  • How do toys teach children which behaviors, futures, and social roles are expected of them?
  • What happens when AI systems learn from biased consumer datasets?
  • How does absurdity make cultural bias easier to see?
  • How do unboxing media, online shopping, and machine learning overlap?
